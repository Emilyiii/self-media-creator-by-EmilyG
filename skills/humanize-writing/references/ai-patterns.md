# AI Writing Patterns Catalog

## Category 1: Generic Openings

### Pattern: "In today's world..."
**Variations:**
- "In today's rapidly changing world..."
- "In the modern era..."
- "In our current society..."
- "In this day and age..."

**Why AI uses it:** Safe, universally applicable, requires no specific knowledge
**Why humans don't:** It's meaningless filler

**Human alternatives:**
- Start with a specific time/place: "Last Tuesday, I..."
- Start with a problem: "I kept making the same mistake..."
- Start with a question: "What if everything we know about X is wrong?"
- Start with a bold claim: "Most productivity advice is garbage."

---

### Pattern: "It is important to note that..."
**Variations:**
- "It is worth mentioning that..."
- "It should be noted that..."
- "One important thing to remember is..."
- "It's crucial to understand that..."

**Why AI uses it:** Hedging, avoids direct statements
**Why humans don't:** If it's important, just say it

**Human alternatives:**
- Direct statement: "Here's the thing:"
- Emphasis: "The real issue is..."
- Just say it without preamble

---

## Category 2: Excessive Signposting

### Pattern: "First... Second... Third..."
**Variations:**
- "Firstly... Secondly... Thirdly..."
- "Step 1... Step 2... Step 3..."
- Numbered lists in prose

**Why AI uses it:** Clear structure, easy to generate
**Why humans don't:** Robotic, predictable, boring

**Human alternatives:**
- Natural flow without labels
- Varied transitions: "Here's what worked," "Then I tried," "The surprise was"
- Let structure emerge from content

---

### Pattern: "Let's dive in..."
**Variations:**
- "Let's get started..."
- "Without further ado..."
- "Let's jump right in..."
- "So let's begin..."

**Why AI uses it:** Transitional filler
**Why humans don't:** Wasted words, everyone skips this

**Human alternatives:**
- Just start
- Use a subheading
- One word: "Okay." or "So."

---

## Category 3: Overly Formal Language

### Pattern: "One could argue that..."
**Variations:**
- "It could be said that..."
- "Some might suggest..."
- "There are those who believe..."

**Why AI uses it:** Avoids commitment, hedges bets
**Why humans don't:** Weakens the point

**Human alternatives:**
- State your position: "I think..."
- Acknowledge nuance: "This isn't black and white, but..."
- Be direct: "Here's what I believe:"

---

### Pattern: "Furthermore... Moreover..."
**Variations:**
- "Additionally..."
- "In addition..."
- "What's more..."

**Why AI uses it:** Academic writing training
**Why humans don't:** Stiff, outdated

**Human alternatives:**
- "Plus,"
- "And here's the kicker:"
- "But wait, there's more:" (playful)
- Just start the next sentence

---

## Category 4: Vague Generalizations

### Pattern: "Many people..."
**Variations:**
- "Some experts..."
- "Research shows..."
- "Studies indicate..."
- "It is widely believed..."

**Why AI uses it:** No need for specific citations
**Why humans don't:** Unconvincing, lazy

**Human alternatives:**
- Specific examples: "My colleague Sarah..."
- Personal experience: "In my 5 years of..."
- Named sources: "According to Dr. Chen's 2023 study..."
- Admit uncertainty: "I don't have data on this, but anecdotally..."

---

### Pattern: "Various factors contribute to..."
**Variations:**
- "There are many reasons why..."
- "Multiple elements play a role..."
- "A combination of factors..."

**Why AI uses it:** Vague is safe
**Why humans don't:** Says nothing

**Human alternatives:**
- List the actual factors
- Focus on the most important one
- Use a specific example

---

## Category 5: Perfect Structure

### Pattern: Uniform Paragraph Length
**AI behavior:** Every paragraph 3-5 sentences
**Human reality:** Paragraphs vary naturally

**Fix:**
- Mix short and long paragraphs
- Use one-sentence paragraphs for emphasis
- Let content dictate length

---

### Pattern: Predictable Patterns
**AI behavior:**
- Introduction → 3 body points → Conclusion
- Problem → Solution → Benefits
- Always balanced arguments

**Human reality:**
- Sometimes digress
- Occasionally unbalanced
- Structure serves content, not vice versa

**Fix:**
- Allow natural flow
- Include relevant tangents
- Don't force symmetry

---

## Category 6: AI Tells

### Overuse of Adverbs
**AI pattern:** "Interestingly," "Surprisingly," "Importantly,"
**Human fix:** Let the content be interesting/surprising/important

### Passive Voice
**AI pattern:** "It was decided that..." "Mistakes were made..."
**Human fix:** "We decided..." "I made mistakes..."

### Lack of Contractions
**AI pattern:** "do not" "cannot" "it is"
**Human fix:** "don't" "can't" "it's"

### Perfect Grammar
**AI pattern:** No fragments, no run-ons, no stylistic choices
**Human fix:** Occasional fragments. For emphasis. Or rhythm.

---

## Quick Detection Checklist

When reading text, check for:

- [ ] Opens with "In today's..." or similar
- [ ] Uses "First... Second... Third..."
- [ ] Contains "It is important to note..."
- [ ] Has "Furthermore" or "Moreover"
- [ ] Says "Many people..." without specifics
- [ ] All paragraphs similar length
- [ ] No contractions
- [ ] Perfect grammar throughout
- [ ] Generic examples
- [ ] Predictable structure

**Score:**
- 0-2: Probably human
- 3-5: Might be AI-assisted
- 6+: Likely AI-generated
